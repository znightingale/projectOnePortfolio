my five favorite trees

  • Redwood - Sequoia sempervirens

    I have lived close to redwoods my entire life, and for that reason I have
    a close connection to them. They like coastal areas with lots of fog, and
    it makes redwood forests always look magical and mysterious to me.
    The way they reach so high into the sky makes me feel small, and I think
    it is important as a human to remember how big and how important nature is.

  • Ginkgo - Ginkgo biloba

    I learned about Ginkgo trees in a botany class I took a year ago, and
    they are so fascinating! These trees are a part of the gymnosperm family
    alongside many types of palms and pine trees. However, Ginkgos do not
    appear naturally in the wild anymore, and have no surviving relatives,
    meaning that they are truly one of a kind and are only still around
    because humans have planted them for thousands of years!

  • Bristlecone Pine - Pinus longaeva

    The Ancient Bristlecone Pine Forest is perhaps one of the coolest places
    I have ever heard about, and it is home to none other than the Bristlecone
    Pines. These trees are some of the oldest living organisms on Earth, with
    some of them having been around for 5000 years! It fills me with wonder to
    think about he things they have lived through, and someday I hope to visit
    them and just sit in the presence of such wisdom.

  • Canyon Live Oak - Quercus chrysolepis

    Canyon Live Oaks are everywhere in California. I come across them all the
    time, and I always think they are beautiful. They remind me of the natural
    landscape of the west coast and the relationship the native people had
    with their environment. They would burn grasses and small shrubs that
    surrounded these oaks regularly, keeping the ecosystem balanced and
    healthy.

  • Azores Juniper - Juniperus brevifolia

    Juniper trees come in many variations, but this particular one looks really beautiful
    to me. The way it stretches out and balances its long branches feels to me like
    a perfect harmony. It's also interesting to see other types of Juniper trees
    and compare them to this one!
