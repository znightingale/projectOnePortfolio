My five favorite trees

  • Redwood - Sequoia sempervirens

    I have lived close to redwoods my entire life, and for that reason I have a close connection to them. The fog in redwood forests always looks magical and mysterious to me, and it's amazing to see how tall they stretch into the sky!

  • Ginkgo - Ginkgo biloba

    Ginkgos do not appear naturally in the wild anymore, and have no surviving relatives, meaning that they are truly one of a kind and are only still around because humans have planted them for thousands of years!

  • Bristlecone Pine - Pinus longaeva

    These trees are some of the oldest living organisms on Earth, with some of them having been around for 5000 years! Someday I hope to visit them in the California deserts and sit in the presence of their wisdom.

  • Canyon Live Oak - Quercus chrysolepis

    Oaks remind me of the relationship the native people of the west coast had with their environment. They would burn grasses and small shrubs that surrounded these oaks regularly, keeping the ecosystem healthy.

  • Azores Juniper - Juniperus brevifolia

    Juniper trees come in many variations, but this particular one looks really beautiful to me. I love the way it stretches out and balances its long branches.
